The problem

Rigorous simulators don't teach you anything

Tools like Aspen Plus or DWSIM are powerful. But they hand you a blank canvas, expect you to already know the process, and produce results without ever explaining why a unit operation belongs in the flowsheet, what assumptions are being made, or what happens if a stream is misconfigured.

For students and engineers learning process design for the first time, this is a wall. You can't run a simulation without already knowing what you're doing — and the tool won't tell you what you're missing.

No explanation of why a unit is needed
Hidden assumptions and opaque property methods
No guidance when a topology is incomplete or wrong
Optimization is a separate expert skill, not integrated
The ChemFlow approach

Visualization + AI as a learning layer

ChemFlow is built around the idea that understanding a process and simulating it should happen at the same time. Every action surfaces an explanation.

01
Draw the process visually
Place unit operations on a canvas, connect streams, and see the topology take shape. The graph is validated before any calculation runs — missing specs and structural errors are flagged immediately.
02
Ask the AI copilot why
Click any unit and ask the LLM copilot to explain its role, what happens if it's removed, or why a warning was raised. Every answer includes the assumption made, the confidence level, and a recommended action.
03
Run transparent calculations
The engineering engine shows exactly which model produced each result — simplified balance, Antoine flash, or surrogate prediction — and flags where a rigorous simulator would be needed for real decisions.
04
Understand what drives the outcome
Surrogate optimization tells you not just the best operating point but which variables matter most and why — making the engineering logic visible rather than hiding it inside a black-box solver.

Teaching example

CO₂ to Methanol flowsheet

The included demo flowsheet models a simplified CO₂ hydrogenation pathway to methanol: both feeds mixing, compression to reaction pressure, pre-heating, stoichiometric reaction, cooling, and flash separation.

It is intentionally simplified — ideal-mixture assumptions, stoichiometric conversion, and Antoine K-value flash — to serve as a transparent teaching case rather than a rigorous simulation. The AI copilot will tell you exactly which assumptions were applied and where more careful modelling would be needed.

Capabilities

Four connected pillars

Each layer builds on the last — from visual design to AI-guided optimization.

Visual Flowsheeting
Drag-and-drop unit operations onto a React Flow canvas. Connect streams, edit parameters in-panel, and load pre-built example flowsheets. The graph topology is validated before any calculation runs.
React Flow JSON export Graph validation
Engineering Checks
Structured mass balances, component-weighted Cp, Antoine flash K-values, phase-change detection, and recycle convergence via successive substitution. Results surface as a stream table with named KPIs.
FastAPI NumPy Recycle convergence
AI Copilot
Every copilot card is generated by an LLM — explain unit roles, map solver warnings to recommended actions, flag structural heuristic violations. Reason, assumption, confidence, and recommended action are structured fields on every response, so the model's reasoning is always visible.
LLM-generated cards Structured reasoning Confidence scores
Surrogate Optimization
Run parameter sweeps to build a run history, train a RandomForest surrogate per KPI, and optimize over reactor temperature, pressure, feed ratios, and split fractions without calling the full solver each time.
RandomForest Sweep → train → optimize JSONL run history
Explainable Insights
Feature importance scores from each trained surrogate are returned alongside the optimization result. The copilot interprets which decision variables dominate purity, conversion, or duty — and why the recommended operating point is preferred over alternatives. No hidden decisions.
Feature importance Plain-language summaries No black-box outputs

Known scope limits

No rigorous thermodynamics (DWSIM / Aspen-level)
Antoine + ideal-mixture only; results are teaching estimates
No distillation, absorption, or rate-based separation
Flash drum is the current phase-split model
105 chemical compounds with built-in property data
Unknown components still accepted; results flagged with unknown_component_properties
Surrogate trained on random samples, not full Pareto front
Single-objective optimize only in current release
Inline stream inspector overlays not yet wired up
Render Free Tier — may sleep after inactivity
Expect a ~20s cold start; reload if the page times out
Next

Roadmap

Planned extensions that strengthen the engineering core and push the optimization further.

Rigorous validation
Planned

DWSIM sidecar for rigorous flash and distillation

Validated cases will be handed off to a DWSIM worker service, results imported back, and the deviation between simplified and rigorous KPIs displayed side-by-side. This turns the surrogate into a meaningful approximation of a real simulation rather than just of a simplified one.

  • Export/import round-trip to DWSIM
  • Simplified vs. rigorous KPI comparison panel
  • Rigorous dataset generation for surrogate training
Stronger process models
Planned

Tighter energy balances and rated-unit operations

Mixer and reactor energy balances will be tightened. Distillation column and absorption unit types are on the scope list, along with continued expansion of the 105-compound property database beyond current species.

  • Energy balance closure on mixers and reactors
  • Column unit type (shortcut / Fenske–Underwood)
  • Expanded property database
Multi-objective optimization
Planned

Pareto exploration across competing KPIs

Current optimization is single-objective. Pareto front generation will let users explore purity vs. utility cost, conversion vs. CO₂ intensity, and other engineering trade-offs with visual exploration of the non-dominated front.

  • Pareto sweep and front visualization
  • Trade-off interpretation cards from the copilot
  • Per-run SHAP-style local explanations
Surrogate backends
Research

Gradient-boosted and neural surrogate models

The current RandomForest backend will be extended with XGBoost and a lightweight neural surrogate, allowing comparison between surrogate accuracy and training cost as the run history grows.

  • XGBoost and MLP backends
  • Surrogate accuracy vs. run-count curves
  • Active learning sampling strategy
